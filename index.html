Placement Synopsis

Industry Context

The Kimberley region represents a biodiversity hotspot with unique flora and rich traditional medicinal knowledge, yet significant gaps exist between documented Indigenous uses and modern phytochemical characterization. The Institute for Biomedicine and Glycomics (formerly GRIDD) operates NatureBank, Australia's largest natural product collection, and focuses on early-stage drug discovery for cancers, infectious diseases (malaria, tuberculosis), and antimicrobial resistance.

Professor Miaomiao Liu's research program integrates native mass spectrometry, artificial intelligence, and natural product chemistry for molecular target identification. This placement addressed a critical bottleneck: the need for systematic literature review to expand the research portfolio beyond current bark-based analgesic studies, enabling competitive grant applications while maintaining laboratory productivity.

Placement Role

Research Assistant position within the Institute for Biomedicine and Glycomics, conducting independent literature review under academic supervision. Work conducted primarily remotely with occasional on-site presence at N75 building, Nathan campus.

Core Activities:

  • Systematic literature searching across CSIRO Publishing, MEDLINE, Springer Nature Link, and Web of Science
  • Database population with stringent selection criteria (preparative chromatography and NMR structural elucidation required)
  • Cross-referencing scientific literature with King & Horsfall (2023) traditional use documentation
  • ChemDraw structure visualization for secondary metabolites
  • SwissADME computational analysis for drug-like properties
  • Python scripting for large-scale chemical data processing

Deliverables
